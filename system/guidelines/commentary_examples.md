# Commentary examples (sanitized gallery)

Illustrative report write-ups to imitate for STRUCTURE and VOICE. **All figures are
placeholders** (`$X`, `N`, `A → B`) — these show *phrasing*, never facts. Every real
number in a report must come from the computed FACTS, not from here.

## Example — monthly awareness summary with an operational caveat

A mid-month staffing/budget change reduced boosted-post and some targeting activity from
mid-[month].

In [month], paid media continued on [Meta and Google], [Snap] ran on an ad credit, and
[TV and radio] were live with all [N] PSAs; the focus was driving people to the
[priority landing page].

Media investment and impressions stayed relatively flat MoM, but we saw an increase in
site visits and users.

Month-over-month investment decreased slightly from $X to $Y:

- Impressions increased from [A] to [B]
- Site visits increased from [A] to [B]
- Website users increased from [A] to [B]
- Total PDF downloads decreased from [A] to [B]
- [Safety-report] clicks increased from [A] to [B]

Only [N] post could be boosted on [Meta] for "[awareness theme]" on [date] due to the
staffing change. Spend of $X reached [N] people, drove [N] impressions and [N] link
clicks to [campaign] resources.

**Why it works — patterns to reuse:**

- Open with any operational or external factor (staffing, budget, flight timing) that
  explains a change in tactics, so the metric movements have context instead of reading
  as unexplained swings.
- State *what was live* this month — channels, what was in market, the focus — before the
  numbers.
- Lead the numbers with the *shape of the story* ("investment and impressions flat, but
  visits and users up"), then a clean MoM bullet list, each line "metric
  [increased/decreased] from A to B".
- Close a notable single tactic with a spend → outcomes chain (spend → people reached →
  impressions → link clicks).
